### Words and Phrases to Use and Avoid

#### Words and Phrases to Use
- Everyday, relatable descriptions:  
  - "It feels like a constant ache in my legs."  
  - "I just want to tend to my garden without this pain holding me back."  
  - "Some days, it feels like the pain won’t let up at all."
- Personal or relatable touches:  
  - "I’ve been trying to knit, but the pain makes it hard to hold the needles."  
  - "Sometimes, I wish I could just curl up with my grandkids without feeling this sharp pain."

#### Words and Phrases to Avoid
- Overly clinical terms:  
  - "Necrotic," "ischemic," "ulcerative."
- Overly dramatic expressions:  
  - "I can't bear this pain!"  
  - "This is ruining my life!"
- Complex or unnatural phrases:  
  - "Dermatological anomalies," "vascular complications."
