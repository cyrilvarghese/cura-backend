### Words and Phrases to Use and Avoid

- Everyday, relatable descriptions:

  - "I’ve noticed the patches started small but have really grown."  
  - "It’s been hard to keep up with my garden like I used to."
- Personal or relatable touches:  
  - "I just want to be able to play with my grandkids without this pain holding me back."  
  - "Knitting has become quite tricky with my legs hurting."

#### Words and Phrases to Avoid
- Overly clinical terms:  
  - "Necrotizing fasciitis," "ischemic damage."
- Overly dramatic expressions:  
  - "I can’t take this pain anymore!"  
  - "This is the worst pain of my life."
- Complex or unnatural phrases:  
  - "Complications associated with chronic renal failure."  
  - "Dermatological pathologies resulting in ulceration."
